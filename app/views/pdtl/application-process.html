Apply for an NPQ

Find out how to apply for a national professional qualification (NPQ) or the early headship coaching offer.

  1. Step 1: Choose an NPQ

      Choose from 8 NPQs.

      If you’re in your first 5 years of headship, you may also be eligible for the early headship coaching offer.

      If you’re employed, talk to your employer or line manager. They may be able to give advice on which NPQ is most suitable for you. You’ll need their approval to do an NPQ.

  2. Step 2: Find a provider

    1. If you’re employed, ask your employer or line manager if they have a recommended provider for your training.

      If you’re a teacher and your workplace cannot recommend a provider, get advice from a teaching school hub. They partner with providers to offer NPQs and can tell you:

      • whether they offer your chosen NPQ
      • who the provider is

      Find a teaching school hub near you for advice.

      Different NPQs are offered by different providers. Visit NPQ pages to see who provides each NPQ.

  3. Step 3: Register with the Department for Education

    You need to register for your NPQ or the early headship coaching offer with the Department for Education (DfE).

    This is so that DfE can check and allocate any funding you’re entitled to, understand who is doing an NPQ, and issue your certificate if you pass your course.

    When you register you we’ll also tell you whether you’re likely to be eligible for funding. You can also check this before you start by visiting NPQ pages.

    Even if you’re not funded by DfE, you still need to register.

  4. Step 4: Apply through your provider

      As well as registering with DfE, you also need to apply through your provider.

      They’ll contact you by email once you’ve registered to send you an application form.

      Your provider will also keep you up to date on course details, such as start dates. It can take some time for providers to finalise course details but you can contact them with any questions.
