Apply for an NPQ

Find out how to apply for a national professional qualification (NPQ) or the early headship coaching offer.

  1. Step 1: Choose an NPQ

      Choose from 8 NPQs or the early headship coaching offer.

      If you’re employed, ask your employer or line manager for advice on choosing an NPQ that’s suitable for you. You’ll need your workplace’s approval to do an NPQ.

  2. Step 2: Find a provider

    1. If you’re employed, ask your employer or line manager if they have a recommended provider for your training.

      Visit course pages to see who provides each NPQ and for advice on how to choose between providers:

      • Leading teaching
      • Leading behaviour and culture
      • Leading literacy
      • Leading teacher development
      • Senior leadership
      • Headship
      • Executive leadership
      • Early years leadership
      • Early headship coaching offer

      Alternatively, find a teaching school hub near you. They partner with providers to offer NPQs and can tell you:

      • whether they offer your chosen NPQ
      • who the provider is - you’ll need to know this when you register (next step)

  3. Step 3: Register with the Department for Education

    You need to register for your NPQ or the early headship coaching offer with the Department for Education (DfE).

    This is so that DfE can check and allocate any funding you’re entitled to, understand who is doing an NPQ, and issue your certificate if you pass your course.

    Even if you’re not funded by DfE, you still need to register.

    {{ govukButton({ text: "Register for an NPQ", href: "/start" }) }}

  4. Step 4: Apply through your provider

      As well as registering with DfE, you also need to apply through your provider.

      They’ll contact you by email once you’ve registered to outline how to apply.

      Your provider will also keep you up to date about the course details, such as start dates.

  5. Step 5: Start your NPQ

      If you pass your NPQ you’ll get a certificate which you can access through the teacher self service portal.

      If you withdraw or fail your course you will not be eligible for DfE funding again for the same NPQ.
